E & D

The steps were as follows:

  • Make sure the breadboard works one last time
  • Begin to remove wires from the breadboard and place them into the perfboard one at a time
  • solder all the wires into the perfboard
  • Make sure the perfboard works once all the wires are moved over

The process:

Moving all of my wires from one board to another went smoothly. I was able to solder all of the wires well and moved through that process well. I did have a couple challenges besides my process of soldering and moving the wires into the perfboard but I will go over those in the challenges portion of this reflection

Challenges, setbacks, and solutions:

I had two small setbacks during the process. First, I originally placed my chip in the slot two holes down in comparison to my breadboard. In order to fix this problem I had to place all of my wires in correlation to my chip because I couldn't use the same numbers for rows as my breadboard anymore. This made the process a little more challenging but I was still able to work around it. My second small setback was not soldering every peg of my chip into the perfboard. By meeting with Mrs. Riley however I was able to identify this problem and easily fix it by soldering them into place. Overall I would say this perfboard process went well and I had fun doing it too.
